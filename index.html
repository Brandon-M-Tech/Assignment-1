Project idea

Overview

Creating an app or game that teachers could give to students that would allow them
to see what learning style best suits the students needs. Potentially storing the
results in a database that could then be used at other schools if the child was to
move. The program could also recommend certain activities to give to the child.

Motivation

A 2018 report titled Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA)
details the long term decline in Australian students in Science, Reading and Mathematics subjects
in comparison to Chinese students. Specifically the report details that Australian
students are on average 3.5 years behind Chinese students in mathematics. STEM is a already
underrepresented area of education within Australia, that is a known area to provide jobs
for the workforce of the future.
The purpose of this app/game is to develop a tool that could be used in Australian class rooms to provide
insight to teachers on children's preferred learning styles, which could help close the gap.

Description

This app will be android based and can be downloaded from the Google Play Store.
Students can simply register using a student ID or similar, which will then create an account
for the student where the results of games played will be stored.

Auditory learners learn best by hearing instructions.
Memory games where the student can be asked to memorise as many words or numbers as possible,
by only hearing audio ques.
Asked to recreate a picture after only receiving audio instructions of what it should look like at the end.

Visual learners learn best when they can see the information being taught.
Memory games can also play a part in testing for visual learner attributes.
Having a series of pictures (flashcards) flick past the screen at a certain speed,
then have the student recall as many as they can.
Playing a short video of a task being performed. Have the student perform the task.

Kinesthetic learners like to learn by doing.
Typically having good hand-eye coordination, quick reaction and good motor memory skills.
Games that test these can include interactive and/or reaction based games Coming back to these
games at a later stage, and seeing if the student can recreate the results of the first time
playing, but with only simple instructions, i.e. "Perform game 3 again". This can see if the student
had an easy time remembering the task by performing it themselves.

Combining all these games into a fluent and easy to use app will give teachers
the ability to design learning activities that are catered to each student.

Having a database where all students profiles are kept will allow teachers to simply
search for a students name/ID, and receive a quick snapshot of the student.

Example:
John, #13245
Auditory: 30%
Visual: 60%
Kinesthetic: 10%
Learning suggestions for John:
Use flashcards.
Draw diagrams and maps.
etc.

Alternatively entering a list of all students you have in your class, where you can
then have access to what percentage the class is as a whole.

Example:
Class A = 15 % Auditory, 30 % Visual and 55 % Kinesthetic.
Recommended to teach using study groups and having short breaks for interactive activities.
